Work Experience at Harmonic Bionics, Inc

While at Harmonic Bionics, I designed a series of calibration fixtures and life cycle tests for our robotic exoskeleton. My calibration fixtures allowed us to validate and tune our linear/rotary load cells while my life cycle tests ensured functionality between routine maintenance periods.




Calibration Fixture Specifics:

When designing calibration fixtures, I needed our test process to mimic behavior in the robot. For this reason, I incorporated existing components in addition to load cells, meaning that any nuances introduced by this setup would influence the calibration and ultimately produce an accurate calibration process.

Life Cycle Test Specifics:

As mentioned above, I designed life cycle tests to ensure that actuators would last between maintenance periods. In order to find the torques required for this test, I collected data for a 95th percentile male (the largest patient we accomodate for), and I calculated CG position/weights. I then strapped weights to the robot and collected torque outputs (I then used the maximum values as an upper bound on loading cases). I then designed a spreadsheet that calculated moments of inertia, torques due to gravity, and torques due to inertial mass. I then used this spreadsheet to sweep through values that fell within motor RPM and torque constraints, allowing me to speed up the testing process on an external fixture.
